01 / STORY

I like problems where the easy answer is taken away.

The best thing I built this year runs in production at a legal-tech startup. The most fun things I've built run with no network at all.

At VakilDesk I work in the backend team of a real legal SaaS — Spring Boot and PostgreSQL, a React front end, Python integrations, on workflow automation and document intelligence. I joined as an intern and stayed on full-time. It taught me what production actually costs: reliability, edge cases, and the discipline of code other people depend on.

My own projects pull the other way. A heritage app for monasteries in Sikkim, where the mountains take your signal. A sign-language recogniser that runs its model on the phone, because a conversation can't wait for a server. Different world, same instinct: build for the worst day, not the demo.

My CGPA went from 7.29 to 8.2 across my degree — the honest shape of how I learn. Slowly at first, then properly, once I understand why something matters.
