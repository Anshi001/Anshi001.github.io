01 / STORY

Most software assumes a connection.
Mine doesn't.

I didn't set out to specialise in this. I noticed it looking backwards — three projects in a row where the hard part wasn't the feature, it was what happens when the signal drops.

A heritage app for monasteries in Sikkim, where the mountains take your bars away and the tour has to keep running. A sign-language recogniser that can't send frames to a server, because a conversation doesn't wait for a round trip — so the model runs on the phone. A study planner that keeps working on a train. Different problems, same constraint.

That constraint is a good teacher. It takes away the easy answer. You can't call an API, so you learn what the model actually costs. You can't lazy-load, so you learn what the app actually needs. You stop designing for the demo and start designing for the worst day.

My CGPA went from 7.29 to 8.2 across my degree. I mention it because it's the honest shape of how I learn — slowly at first, then properly, once I understand why something matters.
